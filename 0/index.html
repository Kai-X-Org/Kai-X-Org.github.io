Project 0

In this project we explore some interesting behaviors of our cameras.

Part 1

Below are two selfies I took (forgive my lack of photogenecity). The left one is taken up close, and the second one is a zoomed-in portion of one taken farther away. We noticed that the right one is less distorted than the one of the left, presumably because when taking the camera up close, more of my face is taken in by the edges of my camera, which cause some distortions. When taken far away, my face is mostly at the center of the image, creating a less distoreted picture.

Part 2

We again took two pictures. The view this time is of a garage. One is taken far away and zoomed-in (left), the other is taken up close (right) to a particular position. We once again note the more distorted nature of the close-up picture, where the image sort of bends close to the edges. This is due to a similar reason to the previous situation, where the light is coming in closer to the camera's edges.

Part 3

We take advantage of the preiously considered camera distortion to make the following dolly zoom: where we take pictures of some objects, which each successive pictures taken farther away but zoomed in. Stringing them together, we create a video where it seems like an object is getting distorted in real-time.
